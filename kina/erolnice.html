Shrimp in Rice Paper

Goi cuon or Vietnamese spring rolls are a popular dish consisting of vegetables, noodles, pork, and/or seafood. It's a fresh version of fried spring rolls – some call it salad in a roll – which is an excellent idea for a meal during hot weather. While fried spring rolls are made from wonton wrappers based on flour and eggs, goi cuon is wrapped in banh trang or edible rice paper. You can buy edible rice paper in specialized Asian stores, but also in Croatian supermarkets. The rice paper needs to be briefly soaked in warm water – no more than five seconds – to soften it, and then fill it with prepared ingredients and roll it into a roll. The rolls are eaten cold, so you can prepare several portions in advance and put them in the refrigerator for one to two days. When you're ready to serve the dish, quickly whisk together a sauce of peanut butter, sesame oil, rice vinegar, and soy sauce.
